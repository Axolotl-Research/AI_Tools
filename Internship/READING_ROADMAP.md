# Reading Roadmap

**Purpose:** The [`Library/`](Library/) contains 81 files across 7 categories. This roadmap tells you *when* to read what, aligned to the 8-week program timeline. You are not expected to read every file cover-to-cover. Skim for relevance. Go deep when curiosity pulls you. Return to key chapters as your understanding matures.

**Principle:** Reading supports research. If a reading session replaces actual research time, stop reading and start prompting. The books are reference material, not a syllabus.

**Priority legend:**
- 🟢 **Start here / Read** — High relevance for this phase. Read at least the key chapters.
- 🟡 **Reference / Skim** — Useful when you need depth on a specific topic. Skim the table of contents, return as needed.
- 🔴 **Deep dive** — For filling gaps identified during curation or grill-me preparation. Targeted, not comprehensive.

**Key terms used in this roadmap:**

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Artifact** | Any markdown doc, code file, CSV, JSON, PDF, or agent skill you produce and store in your GitHub repository. |
| **hLexicon** | Your domain dictionary — the terms with specific meaning in your domain that you build throughout the program. |
| **Curation** | The process where a research professional reviews your artifacts and records a decision (Merge, Revise, Defer, or Discard). |
| **Entity-relationship (ER) diagram** | A visual map of the entities in your domain and how they connect. The primary synthesis tool for this program. |
| **Grill-me** | A Socratic examination skill that tests understanding through five levels: Recall, Mechanism, Rationale, Edge Cases, and Synthesis. |

## Reading Strategies

### How to Read a PDF When You're Busy

1. **Read the table of contents first.** Identify the 2-3 chapters most relevant to your current research phase.
2. **Read those chapters.** Skip the rest for now. You can come back.
3. **Take notes in your research artifact.** If a chapter sparks an entity or relationship, that's a finding — commit it.
4. **Don't read linearly.** These are reference books, not novels. Jump to what you need.

### How to Use the MAIA-Substack Posts

The posts are short (5-15 minute reads). They are ideal for:
- **Between research sessions** — when you need a mental palate cleanser
- **Before bed** — when you want something substantive but not heavy
- **When you're stuck** — several posts address meta-learning and problem-solving directly

### How to Use This Roadmap with the Grill-Me Skill

When preparing for grill-me, use this roadmap to identify your weakest area. Then go back to the Library file that covers it. The grill-me skill tests understanding across five levels: Recall (what is it?), Mechanism (how does it work?), Rationale (why was it designed this way?), Edge Cases (what happens when it fails?), and Synthesis (how would you extend or redesign it?). Target your reading at the level where you feel least confident.
