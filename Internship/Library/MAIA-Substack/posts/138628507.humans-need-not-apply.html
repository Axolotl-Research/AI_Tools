The Youtube video below from CGP Grey is almost 10 years old, but with only some slight edits, is still on point. This makes it worth considering — what parts of its analysis were durable and what parts weren’t durable and why?

While some of the specific examples ended up being off — the general ideas are all correct. Although the actual rate of innovation has been slower than CGP Grey implied, so far.

In terms of the biggest idea — that for most jobs we are headed to a world where humans need not apply, this is just part of cultural evolution — the jobs themselves evolve. This isn’t a top down problem to be thought about or solved. This is something where a society or economy will need the creativity and flexibility to invent of a huge number of new jobs we can’t imagine yet. The future composition of human jobs is one dimension of cultural evolution.

I would argue that it is correct that most jobs we know today will be destroyed by automation or innovation in the near future, but this isn’t something that there is a magic solution for. The solution will probably look something like what Trevor Noah described as the hustle, while governance and laws and conventions race to catch up with technology enabled cultural evolution. It won’t be elegant.
