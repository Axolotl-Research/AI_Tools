Overview

In the summer of 2022, Merchant Adventure made its first private company investment in Reduct Inc. The founders of Reduct, Robert and Prabhas, are promising young leaders in programming and design.  Reduct is working on re-imagining how people process and curate video – which is economically significant.  All the usual analytical details lined up.  But with smaller companies the real risks come from a whole host of factors which cannot be anticipated at this early stage.  In Kauffman’s terms, the early-stage private company is exploring the adjacent possible and functionally testing a hypothesis that is un-pre-statable.

Although the goal is for private investments to be profitable, the prospective investment return is a variable that we cannot control, and our standard public market risk control tools: low valuations and long time horizons, don’t work.

We are also not investing in enough promising early-stage companies for the functionally random risks of scaling to be averaged out through many positions, as has become the norm at successful venture investment firms. 

The real return which Merchant Adventure can be sure to earn from investing in private companies is to gain domain knowledge on innovations in economically significant areas.   Our private investment discipline and differentiation is our focus on learning. 

We invest in companies advancing human progress when it’s not in the price. Our primary focus is on public companies, but a company is a company - public or private. 

A Knothole is not a knothole is not a knothole

Each company has it’s own perspective on the future, and we can think of these different perspectives as different knotholes to look through. If one is trying to construct an accurate perspective of the future, you need a number of knotholes in a number of different parts of the wall that holds us in the present.

Public company knotholes come with investor relations groups and presentations and audited financials from businesses which have already reached some minimum scale. These public company knotholes don’t give one perspective on all businesses, and they are legally required to limit their conversations with public owners. Private company knotholes are generally looking at businesses that are subscale or scaling and where the restrictions on information sharing on the status of the business and its challenges are much less complicated.

As a research organization, we see value in being able to look through both types of knotholes because of the different perspectives each offers on the future. As an investment organization we see value in being able to invest in private and public companies because we are looking for expectation gaps and the same human biases are at play in both public and private company valuation.

Slowly Building

Given time constraints and limits to the amount of the portfolio invested in private companies, we will invest in no more than 2 new private companies per calendar year.  Most of our activities in the private company space over time will be follow-on deals with existing investments and learning from those management teams.

This pacing over time is expected to develop a portfolio of roughly 15-20 private holdings, assuming an average holding period of 7-10 years.

We are long-term investors and the private investments are long-term holdings. Building a long-term portfolio of long-term holdings is best done slowly.

Summary Criteria

The key criteria for Merchant Adventure in reviewing private investments are:

1.      OPPORTUNITY: Company pursuing a large target market in a domain of interest to Merchant.

2.      NO OVERLAP: No other existing private company investments with overlapping markets.

3.     DIFFERENT PERSPECTIVE: Target company has a solution that approaches its market or “problem” in a different way.

4.      DEPTH OF KNOWLEDGE & EXPERIENCE: Management team has a depth of knowledge and experience in the area – should have been working on problems in the domain for at least a decade.

5.      ALLOCATION: We can see allocating $1MM or more to the investment over time and that capital is meaningful to the target company. We will not compete to write checks. We are fine being alone in writing a check.

6.      RECIPROCATION: Management needs to be natural reciprocators. Reciprocation is the flywheel for human relationships and creates the back and forth where learning takes place. 

7.      CAPACITY: Have we already done 2 new company deals in the last 12 months or calendar year?
