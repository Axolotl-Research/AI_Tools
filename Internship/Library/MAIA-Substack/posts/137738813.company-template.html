BUSINESS

Use the following questions as prompts in writing the business summary:

  1. Briefly describe the company’s existing business and any relevant history.

  2. Briefly describe the financial model of the business in terms of financial ratios - this can be as simple as DuPont (net margins, asset turnover & financial leverage).. or not.

  3. Why is it able to earn a sustainable profit margin? In terms of unit economics or competitive position. How does the company take one dollar and turn it into two?

  4. What competitive advantages does the company have? What gives it an edge?

  5. What big game or large economic opportunity are they planning to profit from?

  6. What areas of rapid progress are they focused on?

MANAGEMENT

Discuss the following as relevant:

  1. CEO strengths and weaknesses: capital allocation, capex, R&D and strategy

  2. CFO strengths and weaknesses: working capital and balance sheet management

  3. How the culture compounds intellectual capital?

  4. Why are these people running this company?

  5. Why will they be the ones to produce exceptional results?

  6. Does management own shares? How does management work with large shareholders/insiders?

VALUATION

Please make sure to address all the following questions:

  1. What expectations are in the current price of the stock?

  2. What are our expectations for the company?

    in terms of one or more of the following:

    1. based on comparables,

    2. as a sum of the parts,

    3. in terms of normal profitability and growth,

    4. total addressable market and market share.

  3. Why do we think its invisible, neglected or misunderstood?

  4. State whether the company should be valued as a multiple of sales or book value and summarize our long-term expectations in terms of the following:

    1. long term target growth rate

    2. long term target profitability

    3. target valuation multiple.

RISKS

  1. What could go wrong?

  2. Any experts or sources to track on what could go wrong?

FUTURE

  1. Discuss what we are watching for in the next 18 months.

  2. Identify how the expected events relate to our expectations.
