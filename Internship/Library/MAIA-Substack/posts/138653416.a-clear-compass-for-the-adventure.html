Our investment approach is designed to get in the way of the three areas listed below, and other, “misunderstood and mis-priced” phenomena.  The first point focuses on corporations as the relevant entities to understand and invest in, avoiding the circular and cyclical asset allocation madness many go through.  The second point asks whether the way we live in the West reflects a culture or whether the way we live is just how humans live when they have a certain level of health care and material wealth. The Gapminder Foundation would call this living on income Level 4, and assert that our income levels define us more than other factors.  On the third point, the network connects and coordinates the agents in the economy. 

The three key systematic factors are:

 Creation of wealth through corporations.  I am ceaselessly amazed that investors don’t get this.  Around the world, the corporation is the vehicle for wealth creation. But it gets the same respect Churchill gave democracy as the least bad form of government. 

Expansion of Basic Health Care, including maternal and infant care.  The average number of births per woman fell from about 5.6 in 1966 (close to where it had been for all human history) to a level just over 2 where it sits today.  The median family in the world today is 4 people everywhere but in parts of Africa.  First time ever.  The flip side of births is life expectancy.  As life expectancy increased, births fell.  And falling births are a precondition for women’s rights and equality movements. The significance of the global public health efforts which catalyzed this change cannot be overstated.

Exponential expansion of networks.  We thought the late 1990’s internet was big – but that was just wiring a few hundred million fixed machines together.  What’s happening now involves over a billion smart phones, data centers full of servers, a network upgrade to 5G and new generations of autonomous devices.  We haven’t even started yet.  This connectivity is more like the development of a new sensory function than anything else, like eyes during the pre-Cambrian explosion.

In these three points, we have a powerful model of how a single dominant and stable household structure for consumption and a single dominant and stable wealth creation structure have both emerged. This sort of “standard consumption and production model” hasn’t ever existed before on a global scale – and it only emerged in the last few decades enabled by advances in global health.

With the network connectivity, we have a substrate that connects and coordinates these consumption and production entities.  This is what our society has evolved into, and once we discern that reality – we can start to ask where this all leads and how to profit and prosper in a future which will be fundamentally new in many ways.
