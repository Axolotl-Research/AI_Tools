Manufactures in Mexico, what the numbers say.

Mexico has become one of the largest importers of advanced manufacturing (AM) technology, which has grown rapidly since 2020 as companies nearshore to Mexico. Mexican manufacturers are investing in technological advancements to stay ahead of the competition.

However, the total imports of AM-related equipment have decreased by 7% annually since 2017, indicating that the inputs for AM are available domestically. The value of imports from the U.S. has also decreased by 10% compared to 2017, with Mexico's Asian partners taking most of the market share.

According to International Trade Administration (ITA), Mexico has over 200 companies producing advanced materials, with producers clustering in the State of Mexico, the Bajio, and central Mexico regions. In 2021, Mexico imported USD 136 million worth of industrial robots, with Japan, South Korea, the United States, China, and Germany being the largest trade partners.

There is also an increasing trend in the use and import of Automated Guided Vehicles (AGV) and Mobile Robot Automation (MRA) in other industries, estimated to reach a global market value of USD 4 billion by 2025.

Mexico is the seventh-largest global passenger vehicle manufacturer, producing around three million vehicles annually. Around 90% of the vehicles produced in Mexico are exported, with 76% destined for the United States. Mexico is also the sixth-largest manufacturer of heavy-duty vehicles for cargo, hosting 14 manufacturers and assemblers of buses, trucks, tractor trucks, and engines, with 95.1% of tractor trucks exported to the United States.

The United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) requires 75% of a vehicle's content (70% for heavy trucks) to be produced in North America, with core auto parts originating from the United States, Canada, or Mexico. Mexico is also the largest export market for U.S. automotive parts and the fourth-largest producer of automotive parts worldwide, generating USD 94 billion in annual revenues.
