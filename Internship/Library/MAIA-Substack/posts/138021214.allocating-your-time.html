The most valuable resource we all have is our time.  In terms of making positive statements on how to allocate time in research – well that’s impossible because if you are really “doing” research, you are exploring something new – which requires focus and imagination as we have discussed.  The process can’t be precisely defined – but the requirements for useful outputs can.

By focusing on what the endpoints need to be, we can establish some simple questions/rules/principles of when research is a waste of time – shown in the graphic below which originally came from the Dodge & Cox analyst handbook circa 1992.
